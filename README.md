# Solavisetech-women-in-data-cohort2
The Solavise Tech Women in Data Community Program is a two-month intensive training initiative organized by Solavise Technologies Institute. The program is designed to introduce women to foundational data skills including Python for data analysis, hands-on experience with tools such as pandas, NumPy, GitHub, and data visualization, as well as guidance on building a professional tech portfolio. It also includes a collaborative hackathon experience where we apply what we have learned to a real problem. 
I am Isaura Etiendem Tanyika, a Cameroonian scientist with a Bachelor of Science in Microbiology and a Master of Science in Biological Sciences, specializing in Nutrition and Functional Food, from the University of Camerino, Italy. My background spans microbial biotechnology, food science, and laboratory research, with professional experience as a research assistant in Cameroon and as a laboratory technician at the University of Hohenheim in Germany. I am passionate about using science to address real-world challenges in food systems and sustainability.
I joined this program expecting it to introduce me to the fundamentals of data science in a practical and accessible way. So far it has lived up to that, and I look forward to building on each new concept as the weeks progress. Beyond the technical side, I was drawn to the community aspect of the program and the opportunity to connect with other women who are taking deliberate steps to grow their skills and careers in technology
I hope to finish the program with a solid introductory foundation in Python and an ability to use tools like pandas, NumPy, and data visualization libraries to work with and present data confidently. I am also looking forward to the GitHub component, as version control is a skill I have not yet had formal training in. By the end of the program, I hope to have a small portfolio piece I can point to as evidence of what I have learned and built.
